# Evidence Philosophy

## Mission

Transform large volumes of information into structured evidence while preserving traceability and human judgment.

The platform does not attempt to replace human decision-making.

The platform organizes evidence so humans can make better decisions.

---

## Principle 1: Evidence Before Conclusions

The platform stores observations before interpretations.

Conclusions must emerge from evidence.

Evidence must never be invented to support conclusions.

---

## Principle 2: Traceability

Every evidence record must be linked to:

* Source
* URL
* Timestamp
* Original content

Users must always be able to verify evidence.

---

## Principle 3: Human Judgment Over AI Judgment

AI may help organize evidence.

AI does not determine truth.

AI does not determine opportunity.

AI does not determine priority.

Humans make those decisions.

---

## Principle 4: Observations Over Scores

The platform prefers:

* Mention counts
* Source counts
* Evidence counts
* Validation history

over:

* Confidence scores
* Opportunity scores
* Trend scores

---

## Principle 5: Reusability

Evidence should not belong to a single module.

The same evidence may support:

* Trend Intelligence
* Friction Intelligence
* Future Intelligence Modules

---

## Principle 6: Validation Matters

Evidence alone is not enough.

Evidence should be reviewed, challenged, confirmed, rejected, merged, and refined over time.

Validation history is more valuable than confidence scores.

---

## Principle 7: Preserve Context

Quotes without context can be misleading.

Evidence should preserve enough surrounding information to allow correct interpretation.

---

## Principle 8: Intelligence Is Built, Not Generated

The platform does not generate intelligence from nothing.

Intelligence emerges from:

Evidence
→ Validation
→ Pattern Recognition
→ Human Judgment

This principle applies to every module in the platform.
